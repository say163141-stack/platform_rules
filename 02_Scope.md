# Purpose of this Chapter

This chapter defines the applicability boundaries of the Universal AI
Engineering Standard.

It specifies where the standard SHALL be applied, where it MAY be applied,
and where it SHALL NOT be considered normative.

---

# In Scope

UAIES governs the engineering of AI-driven systems, including:

• AI workflow design

• multi-agent architectures

• orchestration systems

• prompt engineering processes

• execution management

• validation

• recovery

• engineering memory

• context management

• token optimization

• architecture governance

• engineering documentation

• implementation protocols

• regression prevention

• quality assurance

---

# Out of Scope

UAIES does not define:

• business processes

• business requirements

• UI/UX

• commercial priorities

• company policies

• infrastructure procurement

• programming languages

• cloud providers

• software licensing

These concerns remain implementation-specific.

---

- n8n

# Conformance

A system claims conformance with UAIES only if:

- all mandatory requirements are satisfied;

- mandatory contracts are respected;

- governance requirements are followed;

- regression protocol is implemented;

- validation protocol is executed;

- engineering memory is maintained.

Partial implementation SHALL be declared explicitly.
